A World from a Sheet of Paper

Talk by Tadashi Tokieda: https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=8p02DtmyQhU

  • A the creases in a folded paper are not random. They satisfy some properties:
    • The number of lines meeting at a point is even

    • The sum of angles must be equal to 360 degrees

      \(\sum_i \alpha_i = 360\)

    • The alternating sum of angles must of zero.

      \(\sum_i (-1)^i \alpha_i = 0\)

  • Poisson's Ratio for isotropic material lies between -1 to 1/2
  • Miura Ori is used for folding satellite solar panels, antennas, and you can use it to fold maps!.
  • A randomly crumbled paper has a negative Poisson's ratio.
  • Paper lies in 2.5D.
